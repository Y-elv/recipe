Description

Honey Glazed Salmon with Citrus Salsa is a mouthwatering dish that combines the richness of salmon with the refreshing flavors of citrus fruits. A perfectly seared salmon fillet is coated with a luscious glaze made from a blend of honey, soy sauce, and a hint of ginger. As it cooks, the glaze caramelizes and forms a delectable sweet and savory crust on the salmon. Topping off this exquisite dish is a vibrant citrus salsa, consisting of diced oranges, grapefruits, and lime, mixed with fresh cilantro and a sprinkle of red pepper flakes for a subtle kick. The salsa adds a zesty and tangy element that beautifully complements the succulent salmon. With its combination of sweet, savory, and citrusy flavors, Honey Glazed Salmon with Citrus Salsa offers a delightful and healthy culinary experience that will leave your taste buds wanting more.

Ingredients

  • Chicken Breast or Thighs: 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts or 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs, sliced into thin strips.
  • Thai Basil Leaves: 1 cup of fresh Thai basil leaves, loosely packed.
  • Garlic: 4 cloves of garlic, minced.
  • Red Chili Peppers: 2-3 Thai red chili peppers, thinly sliced (adjust the quantity based on your desired level of spiciness).
  • Cooking Oil: 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil or any other neutral oil.
  • Soy Sauce: 2 tablespoons of soy sauce (you can use low-sodium soy sauce if preferred).
  • Oyster Sauce: 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce.
  • Fish Sauce: 1 tablespoon of fish sauce.
  • Brown Sugar: 1 teaspoon of brown sugar (adjust according to your taste).
  • Chicken Broth or Water: 2 tablespoons of chicken broth or water (optional, to adjust the sauce consistency).

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and sliced red chili peppers to the hot oil. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  3. Add the sliced chicken to the pan and stir-fry for 4-5 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and no longer pink.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and brown sugar until well combined.
  5. Pour the sauce mixture into the pan with the cooked chicken and stir well to coat the chicken evenly. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Remove the pan from the heat and add the fresh Thai basil leaves. Stir gently until the basil wilts slightly.
  7. Taste the dish and adjust the seasonings according to your preference. If you prefer a saucier consistency, add a splash of chicken broth or water.
  8. Serve the Spicy Thai Basil Chicken over steamed jasmine rice.
  9. Optional: Garnish with sliced green onions for added freshness and presentation.
  10. Enjoy your flavorful and aromatic Spicy Thai Basil Chicken!
